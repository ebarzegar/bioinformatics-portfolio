# Gene and Protein Annotation of Human TPI1

## Overview

This project focuses on the annotation of the human **TPI1 gene**, which encodes the enzyme **triosephosphate isomerase**.

The goal was to explore gene and protein information using public bioinformatics databases and identify transcript variants, protein isoforms, coding sequences, and related annotations.

## Objectives

- Identify transcript variants of the human TPI1 gene
- Retrieve corresponding mRNA and protein accession numbers
- Examine coding sequence information
- Review available SNP and sequence data
- Connect gene-level information to protein-level annotation

## Tools and Databases Used

- NCBI Gene
- NCBI Nucleotide
- NCBI Protein
- RefSeq
- UniProt

## Skills Demonstrated

- Gene annotation
- Biological database navigation
- Sequence retrieval
- Interpretation of transcript variants
- Linking DNA, mRNA, and protein records

## Key Findings

The human TPI1 gene has multiple transcript variants and protein isoforms. In the original analysis, the main transcript and protein records included:

- NM_000365.6 → NP_000356.1
- NM_001159287.1 → NP_001152759.1
- NM_001258026.2 → NP_001244955.1

These records were used to examine the relationship between transcript variants and corresponding protein isoforms.
