# 1. Molecular Docking Using SwissDock

## Objective

The objective of this analysis was to investigate the interaction between melatonin and the SARS-CoV-2 main protease (Mpro) using molecular docking. The predicted binding pose generated by SwissDock was compared with the interaction reported in a published research article.
## Method

The crystal structure of the SARS-CoV-2 main protease (Mpro) was selected based on the published study by Feitosa et al. (2020). Melatonin was used as the ligand. Molecular docking was performed using SwissDock to predict the preferred binding orientation of melatonin within the protein's binding pocket.

## Interpretation

SwissDock predicted that melatonin binds within the binding pocket of the SARS-CoV-2 main protease. Comparison with the published study showed that the predicted binding location was consistent with the reported docking results. Molecular docking provides a computational prediction of protein–ligand interactions and can be used to prioritize compounds for further experimental investigation.
